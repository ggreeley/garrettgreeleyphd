## Undergraduate Research Mentorship

### Mentorship as Faculty

During the fall of 2025, I prepared and submitted a research proposal for the Winona State University Early-Year Research Mentoring Program to develop a literature review with early-year undergraduates (i.e., less than 50 credit hours). My proposal, which focused on leveraging theoretical ideas from collaborative memory research to develop hypotheses about how interactions with large language models influence memory convergence, was selected from a competitive pool of applications. Two students were selected by the program coordinator, but both declined to participate.

### Mentorship as a Graduate Student

Over the course of my graduate training, I had the privilege of mentoring a number of talented undergraduate Research Assistants. Starting in my first year, I supervised the day-to-day research activities for the Research Assistants noted below, each of whom typically committed to between 6 and 9-hours of work per week during the semester. As a graduate mentor and supervisor, I held regular one-on-one meetings, group meetings, article discussions, organized and coordinated data processing, and provided resources on professional development.
